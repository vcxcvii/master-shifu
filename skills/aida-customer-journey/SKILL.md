# AIDA / Customer Journey Framework

## When to Use

AIDA (Awareness, Interest, Desire, Action) is the classic customer journey and funnel analysis framework. It helps diagnose where customers drop off in the purchase process and what to do about it. Use when:

- "Why aren't our marketing campaigns converting?"
- "We have high awareness but low sales — what's wrong?"
- "Map the customer journey for our product"
- "How do we improve our conversion funnel?"
- Digital marketing optimization, campaign analysis, conversion rate optimization
- Sales process analysis, lead generation, B2B customer journeys
- Comparing actual customer behavior against the ideal purchase path

## Framework Structure

### The AIDA Funnel

**A — Awareness**
Customer becomes aware of the product/brand
- **Marketing goal:** Reach, impressions, brand recall
- **Channels:** Advertising, PR, social media, word-of-mouth, search, content marketing
- **Metrics:** Reach, impressions, brand awareness surveys, share of voice
- **Drop-off risk:** Low awareness despite spend → wrong channels or wrong message

**I — Interest**
Customer develops interest and seeks more information
- **Marketing goal:** Engagement, consideration, information-seeking
- **Channels:** Website, reviews, demos, brochures, social media content, YouTube
- **Metrics:** Website visits, time on page, content downloads, video views, social engagement
- **Drop-off risk:** Interest doesn't sustain → weak value proposition, unclear messaging

**D — Desire**
Customer develops preference and conviction to buy
- **Marketing goal:** Preference, brand choice, emotional connection
- **Channels:** Testimonials, case studies, comparisons, free trials, ROI calculators
- **Metrics:** Add-to-cart, trial sign-ups, demo requests, qualified leads
- **Drop-off risk:** Interest but no desire → lack of differentiation, trust issues, risk perception

**A — Action**
Customer takes the final purchase action
- **Marketing goal:** Conversion, sale, subscription
- **Channels:** Sales team, checkout process, payment options, order fulfillment
- **Metrics:** Conversion rate, sales, revenue, cost per acquisition
- **Drop-off risk:** Desire but no action → friction in purchase process, price objection, lack of urgency

### Extended Models

**AIDAS:** AIDA + Satisfaction (post-purchase experience → repurchase/referral)
**AISAS** (digital era): Attention → Interest → Search → Action → Share
**Microsoft's SEE-THINK-DO-CARE:** More granular digital marketing funnel

### Funnel Math: Conversion Rates

| Stage | Typical Range | Example (10,000 aware) |
|-------|---------------|----------------------|
| Awareness → Interest | 10-30% | 10,000 → 2,000 interested |
| Interest → Desire | 20-50% | 2,000 → 800 desire |
| Desire → Action | 10-30% | 800 → 160 purchases |
| **Overall conversion** | **0.2% - 4.5%** | **1.6%** |

The funnel always narrows. The question is *where* and *why* it narrows faster than benchmarks.

## Step-by-Step Walkthrough

### Step 1: Map the current customer journey
- List every touchpoint from first exposure to post-purchase
- Assign each touchpoint to an AIDA stage
- Identify the channels, activities, and costs at each stage
- Draw the funnel with actual conversion numbers at each stage

### Step 2: Diagnose the bottleneck stage
- Calculate conversion rates between each stage
- Benchmark against industry norms (e.g., e-commerce: 2-3% overall conversion is good)
- Identify the stage with the highest drop-off relative to benchmark
- **This is your problem stage** — focus here

### Stage-specific diagnostics:

**Awareness problem:**
- Are we reaching the right audience? (targeting issue)
- Are we spending enough? (budget issue)
- Does our message cut through? (creative issue)
- Is our brand known enough? (credibility issue)

**Interest problem:**
- Is our value proposition compelling?
- Is our content engaging and informative?
- Do customers understand what we offer?
- Are we reaching them at the right time/context?

**Desire problem:**
- Is our product truly differentiated?
- Do we have social proof (reviews, testimonials)?
- Are competitors more compelling?
- Do customers trust us?
- Is price justified vs. perceived value?

**Action problem:**
- Is the purchase process frictionless?
- Is pricing/payment clear and fair?
- Do we have urgency mechanisms?
- Is the sales team effective?
- Is checkout optimized?

### Step 4: Develop stage-specific interventions
- Each intervention must target the specific bottleneck
- Don't recommend awareness campaigns for a desire problem
- Prioritize: Fix the narrowest part of the funnel first (a bottleneck downstream limits upstream spend ROI)

### Step 5: Measure and iterate
- Set KPIs for each stage
- Run controlled experiments (A/B tests)
- Re-measure conversion rates after interventions
- The funnel is recursive — today's customer becomes tomorrow's awareness channel (word-of-mouth)

## Example

**Context:** A D2C mattress brand, "SleepZen," spends Rs 50 L/month on digital ads. They have high website traffic (500,000 visitors/month) but only 1,500 purchases/month (0.3% conversion). Industry average for online mattresses is 2-3%.

**Funnel Analysis:**

| Stage | Count | Conversion | vs. Benchmark | Assessment |
|-------|-------|------------|---------------|------------|
| Awareness | 500,000 visitors | — | — | Fine — high traffic |
| Interest | 50,000 view product pages | 10% | 15-30% for industry | Below average |
| Desire | 5,000 add to cart | 10% from product views | 20-40% from PV | Very low |
| Action | 1,500 purchase | 30% from cart | 50-70% from cart | Low |

**Diagnosis:** Two major bottlenecks:
1. **Interest → Desire:** Only 10% of product page viewers add to cart. Problem: customers not convinced to buy. Possible reasons: weak social proof, no clear differentiator, price not justified.
2. **Desire → Action:** 30% cart-to-purchase vs. 50-70% benchmark. Problem: cart abandonment. Possible: high shipping cost, complicated checkout, comparison shopping.

**Recommendations:**

For Desire (priority 1):
- Add customer reviews and ratings prominently on product page
- Add comparison table vs. competitors
- Add "30-night risk-free trial" badge (reduces perceived risk)
- Create explainer video showing product benefits

For Action (priority 2):
- Offer free delivery (remove hidden costs)
- Simplify checkout to 3 fields
- Add retargeting email for cart abandoners within 1 hour
- Add limited-time "free pillow with purchase" to create urgency

**Expected impact:** Addressing the desire bottleneck (add to cart rate) from 10% to 25% would triple purchases to 3,750/month even without changing other stages.

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Treating all stages equally** — The funnel is not symmetric. Fix the narrowest part first. Pouring money into awareness when the bottleneck is desire is wasteful.

2. **Confusing AIDA stages** — "Our customers know us but won't buy" is a desire problem or action problem, not an awareness problem. Assign the diagnosis to the correct stage.

3. **Ignoring post-purchase** — The funnel doesn't end at purchase. Post-purchase experience drives repurchase, referral, and brand advocacy. Add "S" (Satisfaction) for retention analysis.

4. **Using only AIDA for complex B2B** — B2B purchases involve multiple decision-makers and long cycles. Use an expanded model that accounts for evaluation, consensus-building, and procurement.

5. **No numbers** — "We have a high drop-off" is vague. "We lose 70% of customers between interest and desire vs. the 50% benchmark" is actionable. Always quantify.

6. **Assuming linear journey** — Today's customer journey is non-linear (discover on Instagram, search on Google, buy on mobile, repeat). The AIDA model is a simplification — acknowledge this.

## Interview Tips

- Draw the funnel as an inverted pyramid narrowing at each stage
- Put actual numbers at each stage — the math is what impresses
- The bottleneck diagnosis is the most important part: "The problem is not awareness, it's desire — customers see us but aren't convinced"
- Use the 80/20 rule: "We need to fix the cart-to-purchase conversion because that 30% drop-off represents the most leveragable opportunity"
- For digital cases, ask about CTR, conversion rates, CAC, and LTV — data that feeds the funnel
- Post-purchase: Always ask about repeat purchase and referral — shows long-term thinking
